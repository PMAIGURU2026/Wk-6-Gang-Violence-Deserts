Slide 04

The Resource Gap

Shooting rate per 10k residents minus youth resources per 10k residents. Higher score = more underserved.

📚 Dropout Data: The Bronx has a 7.2% dropout rate — highest in NYC — and 24.3% of Bronx residents have no high school diploma, the highest in New York State. Brooklyn's District 23 (Brownsville / East New York) has a graduation rate of just 19% for English Language Learners. In FY2024, 90% of nonprofit contracts were paid late — Bronx nonprofits were owed $365 million in delayed city payments.

Slide 05

Year-Over-Year Trend

2015–2024 · The historic low, the COVID spike, and the uneven recovery that proves the resource gap is real.

2015–2019
Historic Baseline Low
25 years of declining violence. Community organizations funded. Credible messengers on the street. Youth programs open. It was working.
2020–2021
COVID Spike
Programs shut overnight. Courts closed. Street outreach stopped. DYCD and MOCS cut by 33%. The vacuum filled immediately with violence.
2022–2024
Uneven Recovery
Manhattan recovered to near-baseline. Brooklyn and the Bronx did not. The gap between their 2019 line and today IS the resource desert.

For the New York City Council

Invest Where The Data Points

The boroughs with the highest rates of gun violence are the same boroughs with the highest dropout rates, the fewest youth programs, and the most community organizations doing city work without being paid. This is not a coincidence — it is a policy gap, and it is fixable.

01
Fund 50 new youth program sites in the 10 highest gap-score zip codes in the Bronx and Brooklyn. Not studies. Not task forces. Sites. Staff. People on the ground.
02
Mandate annual Resource Gap Score reporting by borough, published to NYC Open Data — so this Council and every New Yorker can see exactly where the city is falling short, every year.
03
Eliminate the nonprofit payment backlog. 90% of contracts were late in FY2024. Bronx nonprofits were owed $365M. Organizations cannot hold communities together without being paid.
